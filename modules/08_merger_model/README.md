# 08. Merger Model

## Objective

Build a timed accretion / dilution model that mirrors a classic investment-banking interview exercise: purchase premium, consideration mix, financing drag, synergies, pro forma EPS, and sensitivity work.

## Files

- `SPGI_TRU_Merger_Model_Full_Practice.xlsx`
- `SPGI_TRU_Merger_Model_Full_Answer.xlsx`

This module follows the extended workbook sequence after the LBO. The case is hypothetical and is meant to train merger-model mechanics rather than comment on a live strategic transaction.

## Workbook Map

- `Assumptions`: premium, mix, financing rate, tax rate, and synergy assumptions
- `Buyer`: standalone buyer market and financial snapshot
- `Target`: standalone target market and financial snapshot
- `Deal_Structure`: equity purchase, EV bridge, cash/stock mix, debt raised, and exchange ratio
- `Accretion_Dilution`: pro forma NI, shares, EPS, and accretion / dilution bridge
- `Sensitivities`: premium versus synergy outcome grid
- `Output`: quick pitch-style summary
- `Checks`: population and structural control tests

## Useful Skills Covered

- building a basic accretion / dilution case from standalone company statistics
- translating consideration mix into new debt and shares issued
- explaining why synergies and financing drag change pro forma EPS
- framing premium, exchange ratio, and EPS output in banker language
- pressure-testing a merger case across premium and synergy assumptions

## Practice Depth

The practice workbook expects the learner to build the full pro forma bridge, not just fill in a premium and read the answer. It is designed to feel like a short-form strategic M&A modeling test.

## Completion Standard

- implied enterprise value is populated correctly
- pro forma EPS and accretion / dilution are numeric
- the sensitivity grid works
- `Checks` reads `OK`

## Suggested Time

- first pass: `60-90` minutes
- timed rebuild: `45-60` minutes
