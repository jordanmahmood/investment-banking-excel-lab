# 03. Comparable Companies

## Objective

Build a public trading comps set the way an analyst would actually use it: peer rationale, enterprise-value bridge, LTM metrics, trading multiples, and an implied valuation read-through.

## Files

- In this module: `Information_Services_Trading_Comps_Blank.xlsx`
- Answer key: `../../answer_keys/Information_Services_Trading_Comps_Answer.xlsx`

## Workbook Map

- `Universe`: peer inclusion rationale
- `Market_Data`: price, share count, EV bridge, and LTM metrics
- `Output`: range statistics and implied TransUnion valuation
- `Checks`: basic population and reasonableness tests
- `Source_Notes`: source-date and methodology support

## Useful Skills Covered

- defending a peer set, not just assembling one
- bridging from market capitalization to enterprise value
- calculating revenue, EBITDA, EBIT, and P/E multiples
- spotting outliers and explaining why median often matters more than mean
- translating peer trading ranges into an implied valuation frame for the target

## Practice Depth

The guided workbook is intentionally more than a blank shell. It forces the learner to complete the EV bridge, rebuild the multiple outputs, and interpret what the peer set says about TransUnion rather than stopping at arithmetic.

## Completion Standard

- all enterprise values and trading multiples populate correctly
- output statistics are formula-driven
- the implied valuation block for TransUnion is complete
- you can defend why each peer belongs or does not belong

## Suggested Time

- first pass: `60-75` minutes
- timed refresh from a fresh market date: `45-60` minutes
